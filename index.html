About

I am a Research Fellow in the School of Physics at the University of Melbourne and a member of the ARC Centre of Excellence for Dark Matter Particle Physics. My research focuses on particle dark matter phenomenology, with an emphasis on the direct detection of dark matter. I am also broadly interested in searches for physics beyond the Standard Model using coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS).

Research Interests

My work sits at the intersection of particle physics, nuclear physics, and astrophysics, asking what the next generation of underground detectors can tell us about dark matter and neutrinos.

  • Dark matter direct detection
  • Coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS)
  • Cosmic-ray upscattered dark matter
  • Migdal effect
  • Solar & atmospheric neutrinos
  • Astroparticle phenomenology
  • Beyond Standard Model physics
